Swiss Parliament Visualization

Prototyping a Visualization of the Swiss Parliament data


About this project

This project represents the final point of the course Applied Data Analysis (ADA) taught by Dr. Michele Catasta at EPFL during the Fall semester 2016. We put into practice the analysis of real-world data, following the whole pipeline from scraping to interactive visualization. By doing so we experienced the real-world challenges that arise in such a project.

It is a common challenge in data visualization to present a big amount of data in such a simple and intuitive way that the user forgets the complexities involved. Our goal is thus to provide a simple yet powerful and playful visualization.

Since the Freedom of Information Act entered into force (on 1 July 2006) a huge amount of data related to political activities is freely available to the public. However, it represents a large number of hardly understandable documents.

We chose to apply our knowledge to data from the Swiss Parliament and we suggest a new way of visualizing the activities of its deputies and increasing the visibility of this data to the public.

To this effect, we suggest an interface giving a light overview of Swiss parliamentary activities.

The Freedom of Information Act came into force on 1 July 2006. It is intended to promote transparency with regard to the purpose, organisation and activities of the federal administration, while guaranteeing access to official documents produced after 1 July 2006.

How to

This visualization focuses on the councilors and their interactions inside the parliament. The landing frame shows the councilors at their respective seats in the parliament, each circle representing a councilor.

Moving the mouse over a circle

If you move your mouse over one of the circles, you will get information about the councilor it represents. You will also notice a change in the opacity of its surrounding points. This is used to display the strength of the relationships between this councilor and his/her colleagues. The more opaque the circles, the stronger the relationship - and vice versa.

On the right panel, you can choose between two type of relationships we considered:

Co-signed motions
The cosigners are the councilors who signed at least one motion proposed by the selected councilor.
Interventions
The speakers are the councilors who spoke with the selected councilor in the same debate.

On the bottom right, a sorted list of those who have a relationship with the selected councilor is displayed and can be navigated to take a look at another councilor.
Once you click on a councilor it fixes the opacity and information associated with him/her and moving your mouse over the other councilors will display some information about them in the lower left corner. To deselect a councilor, simply click on his/her circle again.

Right panel

The right panel is used to display some more in depth information about the councilor. Along with some basic information on the top right, two bar graphs are presented. The top one is a timeline of the councilor's interventions and the lower one shows the distribution of their political interests.
This graph was done by computing the number of motions they cosigned or authored on the different subjects. It is possible to switch to a graph showing only the motions they wrote, and moving your mouse along the bars will display their exact value.

Clusters

If you activate the clusters option, the circles will move from their initial position and form a cluster. This will also activate a list of checkboxes allowing you to split it into multiple clusters by different characteristics such as party, age, gender, etc.

Majority

Once the clusters are activated, you can also activate the majority option. You will be able to move the clusters on the right or on the left of a line in the middle of the visualization. This will then tell you if the majority is on the right or on the left of the line. Frank Underwood would love this!

Colors

You can change the colors of the circles according to the characteristics of the councilors.

Councils

You can choose to remove one or more council from the visualization. This can be particularly interesting when using the majority option.
